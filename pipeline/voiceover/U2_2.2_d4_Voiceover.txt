[Slide 9]
Here's the trap on the applied day. Finding vulnerabilities feels like the work, so students hand in a long list and call it an assessment. But the objective is assess and document, not just identify. A raw list doesn't tell the team where to start — and at a real facility you can't fix everything at once. The assessment only earns its name when every item carries a documented risk level with a justification tied to asset value and likelihood. At Xtensr, 'unlocked server room, exposed USB ports, dumpsters' is just inventory; the value is in grading them — the customer-data server is high and goes first, the dumpsters are low and can wait. Skip the grading and you might cable down cheap items while the high-risk server stays open. Identification is step one; grading and documenting is the deliverable the scenario actually asks for.
