[Slide 8]
Here's the badge-log technique as a mini case study, because it's the subtlest technique. A researcher badges into the Xtensr vault at 4:58 p.m. A normal entry holds the door about three seconds; tonight the sensor logs it open for fourteen. The next morning, reviewing the entry log, the team flags that — a door open far longer than normal is the classic signature of piggybacking or tailgating, someone slipping in behind the badge holder before the door shuts. Notice what's clever here: no second badge was ever scanned, so checking 'whose badge was used' would show one authorized entry and miss the attack completely. It was the door-open TIME that gave it away. Then the team pairs it with footage — pull the vault camera for 4:58 to confirm and identify the second person. So the log detects that something's wrong, and the footage tells you who.
